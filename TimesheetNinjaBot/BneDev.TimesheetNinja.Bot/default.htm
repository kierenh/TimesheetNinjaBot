Timesheet Ninja Bot DEMO

A prototype to explore a conversational interface for time and expenses

It's January 29, 2018, a time when most of us still have to do (fortnightly) timesheets. You are Dorothy McTimesheety, a nimble, tenacious, multitasking employee whose time is in high-demand. There's an upcoming timesheet submission deadline, but not to worry because your company has recently rolled out the Timesheet Ninja Bot (see Chat box below, improvise a little, imagine this is Facebook Messenger)! Go ahead and say Hi, you'll see through some simple natural language interactions how quick and easy it is to submit time and expenses.

Have I submitted my timesheet

When is the deadline for submission

(Another way you might ask...)

Show me my projects

Gets your assigned projects for which you can assign time and expenses

Show me my most recent timesheet

Gets your timesheet for the relevant period, derived from the tense in what you say. Try looking at your previous, latest, or current timesheet

Show me my timesheet [for period]

Gets your timesheet for the specified period

Bot prompts for a period if you leave it off and is great at working out what you mean however you choose to specify the period, such as dd/MM or even "last Thursday"

Have my expenses been paid

Gets your expenses for the prior period (as implied by tense of what you say)

Review my expenses [for period]

Gets your expenses for the specified period

Try leaving off period - Bot understands your intent, prompts for the period

8h on _Wayne Enterprises_

Logs 8 hours to today (January 29, 2018) for the single matched project: AMG7A007 - Wayne Enterprises - new Fusion Power

4 hours on _BMWJ_

Assigns 4 hours to BMWJU099 - RAD Building Cool Stuff (matching projects is a multi-facet fuzzy search of your assignments)

Log 8h to _LexCorp_

You have multiple projects at LexCorp, bot presents these for you to choose and assign the 8 hours

Yesterday I worked 8 hours on _M6662111_

Bot accounts for typos and assigns 8 hours to the project you meant, N6662111 - Australian CTO Office (another feature of the multi-facet search, Levenshtein distance <= 1)

I was not well yesterday

You have multiple sick leave assignments (paid and unpaid), bot presents these for you to choose and assign the time

I chucked a sicky yesterday

Bot even understands the intent of this Ausieism

Bot presents your sick leave codes (paid and unpaid) so you can choose one

I'm going on vacation from 25/01/2018 to 26/01/2018

Assigns 8 hours to annual leave for 25/01 and 26/01

I worked on business development yesterday morning

When you say morning, bot knows you mean 4 hours (08:00 to 12:00)

Resolves business development to BD (if you had multiple BD projects it would prompt for you to choose)

Demo Notes

I'm sure this demo will only have your attention for so long, and chances are you won't be able to guess what the bot can do without it telling you (which it does via menu and navigation cues), but what I really want you to see in action is the natural language processing. Use the sample utterances to rapidly discover what the bot can do, and do take the samples as just that, you should be able to ask in many different ways (as long as it's intelligible). I hope this inspires you to explore conversation as a platform. If something should be as simple as sending a text (or responding to voice for that matter), then maybe it should be, and is especially cool if you want a more human-like interaction. Have fun.

  • The time is fixed to the January 29, 2018. You can look back at time and expenses from December 1, 2017 and there's a few entries for the current period ending January 31, 2018.
  • The timesheet periods for the demo are ~ fortnightly: 15/12/2017, 31/12/2017, 15/01/2018 and 31/01/2018.
  • Assumes your assignment of time and expenses falls within a single period (a simplification for the demo)
  • Bot allows you to enter dates in relevant format for your locale (e.g. Australia: dd/MM, US: MM/dd), and in many different forms - but presenting back is not fully localized so they are in AU format.
  • The term 'now' resolves to the current time, which should normally work, except in this demo where the Space-Time continuum ends at 31/01/2018 - don't panic :S
  • What I found enhanced LUIS extraction/recognition of projects was to really emphasize it, ironically, using the markdown emphasize syntax: _top secret project_ Of course it was the same effect if using other conventions, like @mention or #hashtag, but those are both taken and in messenger (or any chat context) they do have their own meaning. Samples:
    • Assign 8h to _project 404_
    • 8h on _RAD_ yesterday
  • There's twenty three projects that you can assign time to. The Timesheet Ninja Bot can help you discover these, "show me my projects", but to "show off" some of the features of the bot it does help to have some familiarity with your assigned projects. In the real world you know what you're working on, maybe not an exact code, or description, but hopefully at least a client or project code name). For example, if you know your assigned projects, you can simply say: "Log 8 hours to _Wayne Enterprises_". If you don't know, you might say "Log 8 hours", and the bot will present all of your assigned project codes for you to choose one. Rendering twenty three buttons to the chat window (one for each project), isn't the best user experience, rather a simplification for this demo.
  • The demo is read-only, you'll get feedback when you assign time, but you won't actually be updating a real timesheet
  • If you feel like the bot is being a bit stubborn, try 'help', 'quit' or 'back'

Built-on the Microsoft Bot Framework with Language Understanding Intelligent Service (LUIS) and runs on Azure Bot Service. Use this as an accelerator to create a chat bot to compliment any boring old time and expenses capture entry (that has an API), or just to learn more about bots! Check it out on GitHub Star Fork
